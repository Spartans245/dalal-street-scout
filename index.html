CONCEPT 1 · VPB — VOLUME-PRICE BREAKOUT
COILING
Stage 1 of VPB · Watchlist only
WHAT
Price swings get smaller each day — the stock is coiling like a spring. Volume is also shrinking, meaning fewer sellers are willing to sell at lower prices.
WHY IT MATTERS
Low volume = sellers are exhausting. When supply dries up like this, even a small increase in buying demand sends the price sharply higher. Energy is building. Set an alert — don't buy yet.
BREAKOUT
Stage 2 of VPB · Small position
WHAT
After days of coiling, one strong candle breaks out with volume surging above average. The candle closes in the top 60%+ of its range — buyers controlled the day completely.
WHY IT MATTERS
Volume is the lie detector. A breakout on high volume = real demand, not a trap. The spring released. Buy a small position today. If next day confirms, add more.
HOW VPB IS CALCULATED — SETUP + TRIGGER
SETUP — checked over last 5 days
price_coiling — high/low range < 4% over last 5 days. Energy compressing like a spring.
vol_shrinking — volume < 85% of 20d avg for last 3 days. Sellers are exhausting.
TRIGGER — measured on today's candle
vol_ratio = today's volume ÷ 20d avg volume. How much bigger than normal?
close_pos = (close − low) ÷ (high − low). Did buyers hold the gains? 1.0 = closed at high.

CONCEPT 2 · EMA CROSS SIGNALS (14 EMA × 50 EMA)
The 14-day EMA (fast) crossing above the 50-day EMA (slow) is a trend change confirmation. Three entry opportunities exist around this event.
PRE-CROSS
Partial entry
WHAT: Two conditions must both be true simultaneously: (1) EMA14 is below EMA50 but gap < 0.5% of price AND EMA14 is rising fast — cross is 1 day away. (2) A VPB breakout or weak_breakout has fired on the same candle.
WHY VPB IS REQUIRED HERE: EMA convergence alone can be slow and deceptive — two EMAs can drift toward each other for weeks without anything happening. The VPB breakout is the confirmation of real buying pressure happening right now, on this specific candle. Without VPB firing, the stock stays in TRENDING or COILING. When both signals align on the same day, you have a stock that is coiled, breaking out, AND about to cross — that's a very high-probability setup.
WHY ENTER BEFORE THE CROSS: The cross itself is a lagging event — it is calculated from averages of past prices. By the time EMA14 officially crosses EMA50, most of the initial move has already happened. Entering at PRE-CROSS (when VPB fires and gap < 0.5%) means buying when the fuel is loading, not after the rocket has launched. Lower price, tighter stop, better R/R.

POST-CROSS
Full position
WHAT: EMA14 crossed above EMA50 within the last 5 days. No VPB required. What matters instead is volume on the cross day itself: ≥ 1.5× 20-day average = vol-confirmed cross (+18 pts). Score decays with recency: 1–2d ago (+18/14), 3–4d (+14/10), 5d (+10/8).
WHY NO VPB HERE: By the time the EMA cross happens, the VPB breakout event has already passed — it was days ago (that's what caused the price surge that drove EMA14 up through EMA50). Requiring VPB again at POST-CROSS would mean filtering out most valid crossovers. The VPB already did its job: it created the price momentum that caused the cross.
WHY VOLUME ON CROSS DAY MATTERS: A cross on low volume can be a "fake cross" — just price noise moving averages slightly. A cross with volume ≥ 1.5× average means real institutional buying drove the price through the 50 EMA. That's the difference between a signal and a trap.

PULLBACK
Re-entry / kiss-back
WHAT
After an EMA14×EMA50 cross, price surged (the post-cross move). It has now pulled back to within 2% of the 14 EMA — the "kiss-back". The 14 EMA, which was resistance before the cross, has now flipped to support.
WHY PULLBACK > POST-CROSS ENTRY
▸ Lower entry price — you're buying after the initial surge settles
▸ EMA14 acts as dynamic support — natural hard stop just below it
▸ Better risk/reward — tighter stop loss, more room to target
▸ Cross is confirmed — survived the pullback, not a fake cross
WHY ORIGINAL CROSS QUALITY MATTERS
The KISS bonus (+3 in ALL tab) rewards the better entry price, but the underlying signal quality comes from the original cross event. A pullback after a vol-confirmed cross is far more reliable than one after a thin-volume cross.

CONCEPT 3 · TRENDING — WATCHING FOR AN ENTRY
WHAT

WHY IT MATTERS
Stocks in uptrends are candidates — not trades. The macro direction is right. Now you wait for the setup (coiling → breakout) or the pullback to give you a low-risk entry. Patience.

HOW MAX UPSIDE IS CALCULATED
Three overhead targets are computed — the nearest one above current price is shown in the MAX UPSIDE column.
MM — MEASURED MOVE
Target = price + VPB range height
VPB range = max high − min low over the 5 days before today's breakout candle. This is the compressed energy the stock was storing during the coiling phase.
This is the minimum expected move if the breakout is genuine. Markets tend to travel at least as far from the breakout as the range that built before it. Shown as [MM] in the column.
52W — 52-WEEK HIGH
The stock's highest price in the last 252 trading days.
Why it's resistance: Traders who bought near the 52W high are sitting on losses. When price approaches that level, they sell to break even — creating supply.
Used as target when it is the nearest level above current price. If price already exceeds the 52W high, the ATH is used instead. Shown as [52W].
ATH — ALL-TIME HIGH (5-YEAR)
The stock's highest ever price over the last 5 years. Strongest resistance — most sellers want to at least get back to their peak. Only used if the 52W high has already been broken. Shown as [ATH].
TARGET SELECTION LOGIC
1. Compute all three targets: MM, 52W High, ATH
2. Filter to only those above current price
3. Show the nearest one above — that's the first resistance to reach
4. If price already above 52W High → use ATH
5. If no target above current price → show "—"

The target type is shown in brackets: [MM] / [52W] / [ATH] in the MAX UPSIDE column of the scanner table.
UNIVERSE & LIQUIDITY FILTERS — WHAT GETS SCANNED
MARKET CAP FILTER  ₹100 Cr – ₹10,000 Cr
Every stock scanned must fall within this MCap band. Stocks outside are silently excluded — they never appear in any tab.
BELOW ₹100 Cr — EXCLUDED
• Micro-caps: illiquid, wide bid-ask spreads
• Thin order books — one large order moves price 3–5%
• No institutional following — no price discovery
• Easy to manipulate (pump & dump risk)
• Hard to exit quickly without taking a loss
₹100 – ₹10,000 Cr — THE SWEET SPOT
• Real businesses with track records
• Liquid enough to enter/exit in 1–2 days
• Small enough that institutional discovery is still happening
• Best risk/reward: upside not fully priced in yet
• NSE main board + NSE Emerge (SME) both included
ABOVE ₹10,000 Cr — EXCLUDED
• Large-caps: heavily covered by institutions
• Moves are slow and well-anticipated
• Limited upside surprise — price already efficient
• EMA/VPB signals work but upside % is smaller
• Better suited for long-term index investing

MCAP COLUMN — MARKET CAPITALISATION (shown in scanner, not scored)
MCap = total shares outstanding × current price. It tells you the size of the company, not just the share price.

Why MCap matters more than price: A ₹500 share of a ₹200 Cr company is a micro-cap. A ₹50 share of a ₹5000 Cr company is a solid mid-cap. Share price alone tells you nothing — MCap tells you the full picture.

MCap is also used as a tiebreaker in sorting: when two stocks have the same score and the same upside, the larger-MCap stock ranks higher (more established, lower risk).
